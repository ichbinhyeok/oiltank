# Wedge Focus - 2026-04-13

## The only wedge that matters right now
Do not start with:

- heating-oil basics
- broad underground tank education
- generic environmental fear copy
- broad cost lists

Start with:

- buried tank found or suspected during home sale
- abandoned tank records missing
- oil tank sweep before buying
- pre-listing seller risk when records are weak

Support, but do not lead with:

- remove versus abandon in place
- leak and cleanup uncertainty after discovery

## Winning user state
The winning user is already in one of these states:

1. Buyer under contract, tank suspected, records unclear
2. Seller trying to avoid closing delay
3. Homeowner preparing to list and worried about what gets found
4. Buyer or seller told they need a sweep, closure, or environmental professional
5. Leak or contamination concern discovered during closure or excavation

## Pages that should win first
- `state + buried oil tank home sale`
- `state + oil tank sweep before buying house`
- `state + abandoned oil tank records`

Only after that wedge starts working:

- `state + remove vs abandon heating oil tank`
- `state + leaking oil tank what to do`

## Pages that should wait
- broad home-heating pages
- generic aboveground tank maintenance
- broad insurance explainers
- cost-only pages with no scenario frame

## Commercial focus for the first 4 to 6 weeks
Primary CTA order:

1. `Find a tank sweep or locate specialist`
2. `Get the records and proof checklist`
3. `Find a certified closure or removal contractor`

Secondary CTA:

- `Get a records and closing checklist`
- `See what documents to ask for before closing`

## Narrow operating loop
For the first implementation cycle, every build decision should support one loop:

1. User lands on a state or guide route
2. User identifies their scenario:
  - buyer
  - seller
  - owner preparing to list
3. Page clarifies likely next step and what not to assume
4. Page routes the user to the correct lead form
5. Lead form captures enough context to match the right partner type

## First traction signal
The first real traction signal is not traffic.

It is:

- repeated clicks on `tank sweep` or `records/checklist` CTAs from state and guide pages
- followed by at least a few qualified lead submissions tied to buyer-seller routes

## Operator memo
Do not explain oil tanks from the top. Win the moment when a deal, report, or missing record makes someone ask what to do next.
