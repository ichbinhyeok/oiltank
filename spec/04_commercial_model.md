# 04 Commercial Model

## Commercial thesis
This project should monetize from **high-intent service routing**, not from display ads and not from a generic affiliate stack.

## Primary revenue paths

### Tank sweep or locate leads
Best when:

- the tank is suspected but not confirmed
- buyer or seller needs pre-closing due diligence

Why it matters:

- strong intent
- low-friction next step
- early in the funnel but still commercial

### Closure or removal contractor leads
Best when:

- the tank is confirmed
- records are weak
- buyer or seller needs a definitive next move

### Environmental cleanup leads
Best when:

- leak or contamination concern exists
- state reporting or remediation becomes part of the workflow

## Phase 1 commercial scope
For the first public release, the commercial center of gravity should stay on:

- suspected tank during sale
- records missing before closing
- sweep-first or checklist-first next actions

Closure, removal, and cleanup routing should exist in the model, but they should stay support-layer until the first wedge proves that buyer-seller, sweep, and records routes can earn clicks and submissions without turning the site into generic quote bait.

## Revenue paths to avoid first
- broad affiliate product grids
- manual broker operations
- phone-heavy consult sales
- open-ended project management offers

## CTA matrix by scenario

### Suspected tank
Primary CTA:

- `Find tank sweep or locate options`

Secondary CTA:

- `See what records to ask for before closing`

### Records missing
Primary CTA:

- `Get the records and proof checklist`

Secondary CTA:

- `Find closure or locate help if records cannot be found`

### Confirmed tank
Primary CTA:

- `Find certified closure or removal options`

Secondary CTA:

- `Compare remove versus abandon logic in this state`

### Leak concern
Primary CTA:

- `Find environmental cleanup help`

Secondary CTA:

- `See reporting and closure steps for this state`

## Recommended lead form fields
- state
- zip
- scenario
- buyer, seller, or owner
- known or suspected tank
- leak concern yes or no
- closing timeline or urgency
- email
- phone optional, not required
- consent checkbox

## Routing logic
- `suspected_tank_during_sale` -> sweep partner first
- `records_missing` -> checklist plus sweep or closure partner depending on state
- `confirmed_tank_need_next_step` -> closure or removal partner first
- `leak_or_cleanup_concern` -> environmental partner first

## Sponsor packaging hypothesis
Do not lock pricing too early, but structure the inventory as:

1. state sponsorship on state hub plus route family
2. metro overlay sponsorship where lead density later justifies it
3. premium placement for leak-cleanup routes

## Early economics hypothesis
Assumption only, not fact:

- sweep lead value: moderate
- removal lead value: high
- leak or remediation lead value: very high

The business does not need huge volume if lead quality is sharp.

## Commercial quality gates
- no sponsor placement on pages that do not yet have strong route clarity
- no environmental lead routing unless the page makes the risk and uncertainty boundaries explicit
- no `get quotes` CTA on a page that still needs the user to verify tank presence first

## First-cash model
The first-cash model should be:

1. a small number of routed sweep or records-led leads
2. sponsor outreach once a few pages show demand and clicks
3. later state-level package sales if route-family traction appears

## Biggest commercial trap
Do not send every user to removal quotes.

That destroys trust and lowers lead quality.
